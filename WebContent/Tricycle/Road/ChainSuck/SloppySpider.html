Incompatible Spider



The system was sold with a 24-tooth chainring. The spider for the mid-chainring is not compatible with the 24-tooth chainring.
The arms of the spider extend into the space where the chain rides. The chain cuts a groove into the ends of the spider arms.
Rough edges on the gouged groove cause the chain to stick to the chainwheel.

This appears to be a case of sloppy engineering. The 24-tooth chainring should not be installed on this spider.

With a 26-tooth chainring installed, the chain no longer contacts the spider, and the chain-suck situation is resolved.
However, this results in a higher gearing range, and I'm trying to keep the gearing as low as possible.
Anyway, the 26-tooth chainring is not an acceptable solution because it moves the chainline such that the Cargo Monster frame is now in the way.
The power-side chain is deflected by the frame tubing when in the lowest three or four gears. A chaintube here helps, acting as a poor-man's chain idler.
But the chaintube does not change the fact that the chain is deflected; the extra friction reduces efficiency, and will wear out the parts eventually.
